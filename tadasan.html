What is Mountain Pose (Tadasana)?

1. Starting Position: Stand with your feet together, heels slightly apart, and arms at your sides. Distribute your weight evenly across your feet.

2. Aligning the Body: Lift and spread your toes, then plant them back down. Engage your thigh muscles, lifting your kneecaps. Imagine a line of energy running up from your feet to your head.

3. Engaging the Core: Draw your belly in slightly. Keep your spine elongated, tailbone pointing towards the floor.

4. Arm Position: Extend your arms along the body, palms facing inward.

5. Final Pose: Stand tall, breathe deeply and evenly. Focus on the sensation of stability and grounding.

Remember, the key is not to stiffen but to align and engage your body gently. Avoid locking your knees and overextending; keep a slight bend to avoid strain.

Benefits of Mountain Pose

Benefits:

* Improves posture and balance.
* Strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles.
* Firms abdomen and buttocks.

Mental Benefits:

* Enhances focus and concentration.
* Develops mindfulness and body awareness.
* Practicing Tadasana regularly helps prepare your body and mind for more advanced asanas, offering a moment of calm and centeredness.
